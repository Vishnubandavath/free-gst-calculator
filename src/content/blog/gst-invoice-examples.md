# GST Invoice Examples

Learn how to create legally valid GST invoices for your customers and clients.

## Mandatory Invoice Components

Under GST rules in India, a tax invoice issued by a registered business must contain the following details:

*   **Supplier details**: Name, address, and GSTIN of the supplier.
*   **Invoice Number**: A consecutive serial number (max 16 characters, unique for each financial year).
*   **Invoice Date**: Date of its issue.
*   **Recipient details**: Name, address, and GSTIN/UIN (if registered).
*   **HSN/SAC Codes**: HSN code for goods or SAC code for services.
*   **Description**: Detailed description of goods or services.
*   **Quantities**: Quantity and unit of measure.
*   **Valuation**: Total value of supply and taxable value after discounts.
*   **Tax splits**: Rate of tax and amount of tax split into CGST, SGST, or IGST.
*   **Location**: Place of supply (State Name).
*   **Signature**: Signature or digital signature of the authorized signatory.

---

## Invoice Type Breakdown

### Tax Invoice
Issued by a registered seller when supplying taxable goods or services to another business (B2B) or consumer (B2C). It is the primary document required for the buyer to claim Input Tax Credit (ITC).

### Bill of Supply
Issued by businesses registered under the Composition Scheme or those selling tax-exempted goods/services. No GST is charged on a Bill of Supply.

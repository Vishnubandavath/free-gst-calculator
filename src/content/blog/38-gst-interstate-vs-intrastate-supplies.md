# GST Interstate vs Intrastate Supplies Explained

## Overview

One of the most important concepts under the Goods and Services Tax (GST) system is understanding the difference between interstate and intrastate supplies. The classification of a transaction directly determines which type of GST applies and how businesses must report the transaction in GST returns.

Many businesses make mistakes when determining whether a supply is interstate or intrastate, resulting in incorrect tax collection, compliance notices, penalties, and Input Tax Credit (ITC) complications.

Whether you are a trader, manufacturer, retailer, service provider, freelancer, startup founder, or e-commerce seller, understanding these rules is essential for accurate GST compliance.

This guide explains interstate and intrastate supplies, applicable taxes, examples, compliance requirements, and common mistakes to avoid.

---

# Quick Answer

Under GST:

* Interstate Supply = Supplier and Place of Supply are in different states → IGST applies.
* Intrastate Supply = Supplier and Place of Supply are in the same state → CGST and SGST apply.

Correct classification ensures accurate GST invoicing and tax payment.

# What is Interstate Supply?

An interstate supply occurs when the supplier and the Place of Supply are located in different states or union territories.

Under GST law, these transactions are generally subject to Integrated Goods and Services Tax (IGST).

### Example

Supplier Location:

* Maharashtra

Customer Location:

* Karnataka

Result:

* Interstate Supply
* IGST Applicable

---

# What is Intrastate Supply?

An intrastate supply occurs when the supplier and the Place of Supply are located within the same state or union territory.

These transactions are generally subject to:

* Central GST (CGST)
* State GST (SGST)

### Example

Supplier Location:

* Karnataka

Customer Location:

* Karnataka

Result:

* Intrastate Supply
* CGST + SGST Applicable

# Taxes Applicable Under GST

## IGST

Integrated Goods and Services Tax (IGST) applies to interstate supplies.

### Features

* Collected by the Central Government
* Distributed between states as applicable
* Simplifies interstate trade

---

## CGST

Central Goods and Services Tax (CGST) applies to intrastate supplies.

Collected by the Central Government.

---

## SGST

State Goods and Services Tax (SGST) applies to intrastate supplies.

Collected by the respective state government.

---

# Interstate Supply Rules

A transaction is generally considered interstate when:

* Supplier and Place of Supply are in different states.
* Goods are delivered across state boundaries.
* Services are supplied to recipients in another state.
* Imports and exports are covered under interstate supply provisions.

Interstate transactions generally attract IGST.

---

# Intrastate Supply Rules

A transaction is generally considered intrastate when:

* Supplier and Place of Supply are within the same state.
* Goods are delivered within the same state.
* Services are provided within the same state.

Intrastate transactions generally attract CGST and SGST.

# Interstate vs Intrastate Comparison

| Particulars                | Interstate Supply      | Intrastate Supply      |
| -------------------------- | ---------------------- | ---------------------- |
| Supplier & Place of Supply | Different States       | Same State             |
| Tax Applicable             | IGST                   | CGST + SGST            |
| GST Return Reporting       | Interstate Reporting   | Intrastate Reporting   |
| Revenue Distribution       | Shared Across States   | Shared Within State    |
| Common Example             | Maharashtra to Gujarat | Karnataka to Karnataka |

# GST Implications

## Interstate Transactions

Businesses must:

* Charge IGST
* Report interstate supplies correctly
* Maintain proper Place of Supply records

---

## Intrastate Transactions

Businesses must:

* Split GST into CGST and SGST
* Report supplies correctly in GST returns
* Maintain state-specific compliance records

---

## Input Tax Credit

Proper classification ensures smooth Input Tax Credit claims by recipients.

Incorrect tax collection can delay ITC utilization.

# Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

## What is the difference between interstate and intrastate supply?

Interstate supplies involve different states, while intrastate supplies occur within the same state.

## Which tax applies to interstate supplies?

IGST generally applies to interstate transactions.

## Which taxes apply to intrastate supplies?

CGST and SGST generally apply to intrastate transactions.

## Why is Place of Supply important?

Place of Supply determines whether a transaction is interstate or intrastate.

## Can incorrect classification cause GST issues?

Yes. Incorrect classification can result in notices, penalties, and Input Tax Credit complications.

## Are services also classified as interstate and intrastate?

Yes. Service transactions are classified based on Place of Supply rules.

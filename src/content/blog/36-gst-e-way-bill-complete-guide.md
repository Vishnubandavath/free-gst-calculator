# GST E-Way Bill Complete Guide 2026

## Overview

The GST E-Way Bill system is an essential compliance mechanism under India's Goods and Services Tax (GST) framework. Businesses transporting goods worth more than ₹50,000 are generally required to generate an E-Way Bill before movement begins.

The E-Way Bill helps tax authorities monitor the transportation of goods, reduce tax evasion, improve transparency, and streamline logistics operations across India.

Whether you're a manufacturer, wholesaler, retailer, transporter, or e-commerce seller, understanding E-Way Bill requirements is crucial to avoid penalties, detention of goods, and compliance issues.

This guide explains E-Way Bill rules, threshold limits, generation procedures, validity periods, exemptions, penalties, and best practices for GST compliance.

---

# Quick Answer

An E-Way Bill is generally required when:

* Goods worth more than ₹50,000 are transported.
* Goods are moved interstate.
* Goods are transported for supply, return, transfer, or job work.
* GST regulations require documentation during movement.

The E-Way Bill must be generated electronically before transportation begins.

# What is an E-Way Bill?

An E-Way Bill (Electronic Way Bill) is a document generated electronically on the GST portal for the movement of goods exceeding the prescribed threshold value.

The system generates a unique E-Way Bill Number (EBN), which can be shared with the supplier, recipient, and transporter.

The bill serves as proof that the movement of goods complies with GST regulations.

---

# Why E-Way Bills Matter

The E-Way Bill system helps:

* Prevent tax evasion
* Improve supply chain transparency
* Track movement of goods
* Simplify inspections
* Reduce paperwork
* Improve GST compliance

Proper E-Way Bill management helps businesses avoid disruptions during transportation.

# E-Way Bill Threshold Limits

## Standard Threshold

| Particulars | Limit             |
| ----------- | ----------------- |
| Goods Value | More than ₹50,000 |

The value generally includes:

* Taxable value
* GST amount
* Additional charges mentioned on the invoice

---

# Who Must Generate an E-Way Bill?

The responsibility may fall on:

## Registered Supplier

When goods are supplied and transported.

---

## Registered Recipient

If the supplier does not generate the E-Way Bill.

---

## Transporter

When transportation is arranged and neither party generates the bill.

---

## Unregistered Persons

In certain situations involving taxable goods movement.

---

# Documents Required

Before generating an E-Way Bill, keep the following information ready:

* GSTIN of supplier
* GSTIN of recipient
* Invoice number
* Invoice date
* HSN code
* Product description
* Vehicle number
* Transporter ID
* Place of dispatch
* Delivery location

Accurate documentation helps avoid rejection and penalties.

# How to Generate an E-Way Bill

## Step 1: Login to the E-Way Bill Portal

Access the official E-Way Bill system using GST credentials.

---

## Step 2: Select Generate New

Navigate to the E-Way Bill section.

---

## Step 3: Enter Invoice Details

Provide:

* Invoice number
* Invoice date
* Product details
* Taxable value
* GST amount

---

## Step 4: Enter Transportation Details

Include:

* Vehicle number
* Transport mode
* Transporter information

---

## Step 5: Generate EBN

Submit the information to generate the E-Way Bill Number (EBN).

# E-Way Bill Validity Rules

| Distance                | Validity         |
| ----------------------- | ---------------- |
| Up to 200 km            | 1 Day            |
| Every Additional 200 km | Additional 1 Day |

Validity starts from the date and time of generation.

Businesses should monitor expiry carefully during long-distance transportation.

---

# E-Way Bill Exemptions

An E-Way Bill is generally not required for:

* Certain exempt goods
* Agricultural products under specified conditions
* Non-motorized transportation
* Empty cargo containers
* Notified exempt areas

Businesses should verify current GST notifications before relying on exemptions.

# Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

## Is E-Way Bill mandatory above ₹50,000?

Yes, in most cases goods valued above ₹50,000 require an E-Way Bill.

## Can an E-Way Bill be cancelled?

Yes. It can generally be cancelled within 24 hours if goods are not transported.

## Can E-Way Bill validity be extended?

Yes, under specific circumstances such as vehicle breakdowns or unforeseen delays.

## Is E-Way Bill required for job work?

Yes, many job work transactions require E-Way Bill generation.

## What happens if goods are transported without an E-Way Bill?

Authorities may impose penalties and detain the goods or vehicle.

## Does GST need to be calculated before generating an E-Way Bill?

Yes. Correct invoice values and GST calculations are important for generating accurate E-Way Bills.

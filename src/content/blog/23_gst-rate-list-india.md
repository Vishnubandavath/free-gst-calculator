# Latest GST Rate List India

## Overview

The Goods and Services Tax (GST) is India's unified indirect tax system that applies to the supply of goods and services across the country. Since its implementation on 1 July 2017, GST has replaced multiple indirect taxes and introduced a standardized tax structure based on predefined tax slabs.

Understanding GST rates is essential for business owners, retailers, manufacturers, service providers, freelancers, accountants, and consumers. Applying the correct GST rate ensures accurate invoicing, proper tax collection, successful Input Tax Credit (ITC) claims, and compliance with GST regulations.

India follows a multi-rate GST structure where different categories of goods and services are taxed at different rates based on their nature, necessity, and economic significance.

This guide provides a detailed overview of the latest GST rate list in India, including GST slabs, product-wise tax rates, service tax classifications, examples, compliance considerations, and frequently asked questions.

---

## Quick Answer

India's GST system currently uses five primary tax slabs:

| GST Rate | Category                                     |
| -------- | -------------------------------------------- |
| 0%       | Essential goods and exempt supplies          |
| 5%       | Basic necessities and mass-consumption items |
| 12%      | Standard consumer products                   |
| 18%      | Most goods and services                      |
| 28%      | Luxury and sin goods                         |

Most business services and professional services are taxed at 18% GST.

# What is GST?

Goods and Services Tax (GST) is a destination-based indirect tax levied on the supply of goods and services.

GST consists of:

### CGST

Central Goods and Services Tax

### SGST

State Goods and Services Tax

### IGST

Integrated Goods and Services Tax

GST is collected at every stage of the supply chain while allowing businesses to claim Input Tax Credit on eligible purchases.

---

# Understanding GST Rate Slabs

Unlike many countries that use a single VAT rate, India follows multiple GST slabs.

The objective is to:

* Keep essential goods affordable
* Reduce tax burden on necessities
* Tax luxury goods at higher rates
* Promote economic balance

Products and services are classified according to HSN and SAC codes, which determine applicable GST rates.

# GST Rate Structure in India

India currently follows the following GST slabs:

| GST Rate | Description                    |
| -------- | ------------------------------ |
| 0%       | Exempt and essential goods     |

| 12%      | Standard-use products          |

Certain products remain outside GST and continue to be taxed separately under existing laws.

# How GST Rates Affect Input Tax Credit

Input Tax Credit depends on GST paid on purchases.

### Example

Business Purchase:

₹1,00,000

GST @18%:

₹18,000

Sale Value:

₹2,00,000

GST @18%:

₹36,000

Output GST:

₹36,000

Input Tax Credit:

₹18,000

Net GST Payable:

₹18,000

Accurate GST rate application ensures correct ITC claims.

# FAQs

## What is the latest GST rate list in India?

India currently follows GST slabs of 0%, 5%, 12%, 18%, and 28% depending on the nature of goods and services.

Most professional and business services are taxed at 18% GST.

Most freelancing services such as software development, digital marketing, consulting, and design services attract 18% GST.

## How are GST rates determined?

GST rates are determined based on HSN codes for goods and SAC codes for services.

## Can GST rates change?

Yes. GST rates may be revised through GST Council recommendations and government notifications.

The 18% slab is currently the most widely applicable GST rate for businesses and services.

Incorrect classification may result in underpayment, penalties, compliance notices, and ITC disputes.

## Does GST apply equally across India?

Yes. GST is a national tax system with generally uniform rates across states, subject to applicable laws.

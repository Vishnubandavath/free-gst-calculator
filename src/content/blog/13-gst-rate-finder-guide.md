# GST Rate Finder Guide

## Overview

One of the most common challenges faced by businesses, freelancers, traders, manufacturers, retailers, accountants, and tax professionals is determining the correct GST rate for a product or service. Applying the wrong GST rate can lead to compliance issues, incorrect invoicing, tax notices, penalties, and disputes with customers.

India's GST system contains multiple tax slabs ranging from 0% to 28%, along with cess on certain luxury and sin goods. The applicable GST rate depends on the classification of goods under the Harmonized System of Nomenclature (HSN) or services under the Services Accounting Code (SAC).

This comprehensive GST Rate Finder Guide explains how GST rates are determined, how to identify the correct GST slab, how HSN and SAC codes work, common GST rate categories, and best practices for accurate tax classification.

Whether you operate a small business, startup, e-commerce store, consultancy, manufacturing unit, restaurant, or service company, this guide will help you determine the correct GST rate confidently.

---

# Quick Answer

A GST Rate Finder helps businesses identify the correct GST percentage applicable to a product or service. GST rates in India are generally categorized into 0%, 5%, 12%, 18%, and 28% slabs based on HSN Codes for goods and SAC Codes for services. Correct classification is essential for compliance and accurate tax calculation.

# Understanding GST Slabs in India

India follows a multi-rate GST structure.

The most common GST slabs include:

| GST Rate | Typical Usage                         |
| -------- | ------------------------------------- |
| 0%       | Essential goods and exempt supplies   |
| 5%       | Necessities and selected products     |
| 12%      | Processed goods and specific services |
| 18%      | Standard goods and services           |
| 28%      | Luxury and premium products           |

Certain products may also attract compensation cess.

# Understanding HSN Codes

HSN stands for Harmonized System of Nomenclature.

HSN Codes are used to classify goods under GST.

Benefits include:

* Standardized product classification
* Accurate GST rate determination
* International consistency
* Improved compliance

Businesses dealing in goods should understand HSN requirements thoroughly.

---

# Understanding SAC Codes

SAC stands for Services Accounting Code.

SAC Codes classify services under GST.

These codes help:

* Determine GST rates
* Improve invoice accuracy
* Simplify return filing
* Reduce classification errors

Service providers should use the correct SAC codes in their invoices.

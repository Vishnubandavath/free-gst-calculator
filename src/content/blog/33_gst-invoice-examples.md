# GST Invoice Examples: Templates, Formats & Compliance Checklist (2026)

Creating a GST-compliant invoice is one of the most important responsibilities for businesses registered under the Goods and Services Tax (GST) regime in India. Whether you are a freelancer, consultant, retailer, manufacturer, startup, or enterprise, issuing legally valid invoices helps ensure tax compliance, smooth accounting, and seamless Input Tax Credit (ITC) claims for your customers.

This guide explains everything you need to know about GST invoices, including mandatory invoice fields, B2B and B2C invoice examples, bill of supply formats, compliance requirements, common mistakes, and downloadable invoice templates.

## Quick Answer

A GST invoice is a tax document issued by a GST-registered supplier when supplying taxable goods or services. A valid GST invoice must include supplier and recipient details, GSTIN, invoice number, date, HSN/SAC codes, taxable value, GST rates, tax amounts, place of supply, and authorized signature. Businesses must issue GST-compliant invoices to remain legally compliant and enable buyers to claim Input Tax Credit (ITC).

# What is a GST Invoice?

A GST invoice is a legal document issued by a supplier to a customer that records the sale of goods or services and the GST charged on the transaction.

The invoice serves several important purposes:

- Proof of sale
- Tax reporting document
- Basis for Input Tax Credit claims
- Accounting and bookkeeping record
- Audit and compliance evidence

Every GST-registered business must issue proper invoices for taxable supplies according to GST regulations.

---

# Why GST Invoices Matter

GST invoices are more than just payment requests. They are critical compliance documents.

### Legal Compliance

GST law requires registered taxpayers to issue valid invoices for taxable supplies.

### Input Tax Credit (ITC)

Buyers can claim ITC only when a valid GST invoice is available.

### Audit Readiness

Proper invoices help businesses prepare for GST audits and assessments.

### Accurate Tax Reporting

Invoices form the basis of GST return filing and tax calculations.

### Professional Business Operations

Well-designed invoices improve customer trust and business credibility.

# Mandatory GST Invoice Components

Under GST rules in India, every tax invoice issued by a registered business must contain the following information.

## 1. Supplier Details

The invoice must clearly display:

- Business name
- Business address
- GSTIN
- Contact details

### Example

**ABC Technologies Pvt Ltd**  
Plot No. 12, Hyderabad, Telangana  
GSTIN: 36ABCDE1234F1Z5

---

## 2. Invoice Number

Each invoice must contain a unique serial number.

Requirements:

- Maximum 16 characters
- Unique within a financial year
- Can contain alphabets, numbers, hyphens, or slashes

### Example

INV-2026-00125

---

## 3. Invoice Date

The date when the invoice is issued.

### Example

31 May 2026

---

## 4. Recipient Details

For registered customers:

- Customer name
- Address
- GSTIN

### Example

XYZ Industries Ltd  
Mumbai, Maharashtra  
GSTIN: 27AAACX1234B1ZP

---

## 5. HSN or SAC Codes

### HSN Codes

Used for goods.

### SAC Codes

Used for services.

Examples:

| Item | Code |
|--------|--------|
| Mobile Phone | 8517 |
| Laptop | 8471 |
| Software Consulting | 998313 |
| Digital Marketing | 998361 |

---

## 6. Description of Goods or Services

The invoice must contain a clear description of supplied items.

### Examples

- Website Development Services
- Laptop Computer
- Accounting Software License
- Office Furniture

---

## 7. Quantity and Unit

Applicable mainly for goods.

### Example

| Product | Quantity | Unit |
|-----------|-----------|------|
| Laptops | 10 | Nos |

---

## 8. Taxable Value

The taxable amount after discounts and adjustments.

### Example

Product Value: ₹1,00,000

Discount: ₹5,000

Taxable Value: ₹95,000

---

## 9. GST Rate and Tax Amount

Invoice must show applicable GST rates.

### Example

| Tax Type | Rate | Amount |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| CGST | 9% | ₹8,550 |
| SGST | 9% | ₹8,550 |

Total GST = ₹17,100

---

## 10. Place of Supply

Mandatory for interstate transactions.

### Example

Place of Supply: Maharashtra

---

## 11. Signature

The invoice must contain:

- Physical signature
- Digital signature
- Electronic authentication

# GST Invoice Format Requirements

A GST invoice should generally include the following layout:

```text
Supplier Name
Address
GSTIN

Invoice Number
Invoice Date

Customer Name
Customer Address
GSTIN

Description of Goods/Services

Quantity
Rate
Taxable Value

CGST
SGST / IGST

Total Invoice Value

Authorized Signature
```

Following a standard structure helps avoid compliance issues.

---

# Tax Invoice vs Bill of Supply

Businesses often confuse these two documents.

| Feature | Tax Invoice | Bill of Supply |
|-----------|-------------|----------------|
| GST Charged | Yes | No |
| ITC Available | Yes | No |
| Tax Rate Mentioned | Yes | No |
| Used By | Regular Taxpayers | Composition Dealers |
| GST Amount Displayed | Yes | No |

# GST Tax Invoice Example (B2B)

## Sample B2B Invoice

```text
ABC Technologies Pvt Ltd
GSTIN: 36ABCDE1234F1Z5

Invoice No: INV-2026-001
Date: 31-05-2026

Bill To:
XYZ Industries Ltd
GSTIN: 27AAACX1234B1ZP

Description:
Software Development Services

Taxable Value: ₹1,00,000

CGST @9%: ₹9,000
SGST @9%: ₹9,000

Total Invoice Value: ₹1,18,000

Authorized Signatory
```

This invoice allows the customer to claim Input Tax Credit.

# GST Invoice for Export Transactions

Exports are zero-rated supplies.

A typical export invoice includes:

```text
Supply Meant For Export Under LUT
Without Payment of Integrated Tax

Invoice Number
Invoice Date

Exporter GSTIN

Buyer Details

Description of Goods

FOB Value

Country of Destination

Authorized Signature
```

Export invoices must contain additional export-related declarations as prescribed under GST regulations.

# GST Invoice Compliance Checklist

Before issuing an invoice, verify the following:

### Business Information

- Supplier name included
- Supplier address included
- GSTIN included

### Invoice Details

- Unique invoice number
- Correct invoice date

### Customer Information

- Customer name
- Customer address
- GSTIN (if registered)

### Tax Information

- HSN/SAC codes
- Taxable value
- GST rates
- CGST/SGST/IGST amounts

### Compliance Requirements

- Place of supply
- Signature
- Correct calculations

Checking these items helps ensure GST compliance.

# Common GST Invoice Mistakes

Businesses frequently make the following invoice errors.

## Missing GSTIN

Failure to include GSTIN can invalidate the invoice.

## Incorrect Tax Calculations

Wrong GST calculations may lead to penalties and disputes.

## Missing HSN/SAC Codes

Incorrect classification can create compliance issues.

## Duplicate Invoice Numbers

Invoice numbers must remain unique within a financial year.

## Wrong Place of Supply

Incorrect place of supply can result in wrong tax treatment.

## Missing Customer GSTIN

B2B invoices must include customer GSTIN where applicable.

# FAQs

## What is a GST invoice?

A GST invoice is a tax document issued by a GST-registered supplier showing the details of goods or services supplied and the GST charged.

## Is GSTIN mandatory on invoices?

Yes, GSTIN is mandatory for GST-registered suppliers issuing tax invoices.

## What is the difference between B2B and B2C invoices?

B2B invoices contain the recipient's GSTIN and allow Input Tax Credit claims, while B2C invoices are issued to unregistered consumers.

## What is an HSN code?

HSN (Harmonized System of Nomenclature) is a standardized coding system used to classify goods under GST.

## What is a SAC code?

SAC (Services Accounting Code) is used to classify services under GST.

## Can a GST invoice be issued digitally?

Yes. GST law permits digitally generated invoices with electronic signatures where applicable.

## What happens if a GST invoice is incorrect?

Incorrect invoices can lead to compliance issues, ITC claim rejections, notices, and penalties.

## How long should invoices be retained?

Businesses should retain GST invoices and related records as required under GST regulations and applicable laws.

# GST FAQ Complete Guide

## Overview

Goods and Services Tax (GST) is India's unified indirect tax system that replaced multiple central and state taxes. Since its implementation, GST has become one of the most important taxation frameworks affecting businesses, freelancers, professionals, startups, manufacturers, traders, service providers, and consumers.

Despite several years of implementation, many taxpayers still have questions regarding GST registration, return filing, invoices, Input Tax Credit (ITC), GST rates, refunds, exports, compliance requirements, and penalties.

This comprehensive GST FAQ guide answers the most common questions asked by taxpayers in India. Whether you are a business owner, freelancer, accountant, student, or startup founder, this guide will help you understand GST clearly and confidently.

---

# Quick Answer

GST (Goods and Services Tax) is a destination-based indirect tax levied on the supply of goods and services in India. Businesses exceeding prescribed turnover thresholds must register under GST, collect tax, file returns, maintain records, and comply with GST regulations. Understanding GST helps businesses remain compliant and avoid penalties.

# GST Basics FAQ

## What is GST?

GST (Goods and Services Tax) is a unified indirect tax applicable on the supply of goods and services across India.

---

## When was GST introduced in India?

GST was introduced on 1 July 2017.

---

## Why was GST introduced?

GST was introduced to simplify India's indirect taxation system by replacing multiple taxes such as VAT, Service Tax, Excise Duty, and others.

---

## What are the major components of GST?

GST consists of:

* CGST (Central GST)
* SGST (State GST)
* IGST (Integrated GST)

---

## What is the difference between CGST, SGST, and IGST?

| Tax Type | Applicability            |
| -------- | ------------------------ |
| CGST     | Intra-state transactions |
| SGST     | Intra-state transactions |
| IGST     | Inter-state transactions |

---

# GST Registration FAQ

## Who must register for GST?

Businesses exceeding the prescribed turnover threshold must register under GST.

---

## What is the GST registration threshold?

Generally:

| Business Type     | Threshold |
| ----------------- | --------- |
| Goods Suppliers   | ₹40 Lakhs |
| Service Providers | ₹20 Lakhs |

Special category states may have different limits.

---

## Is GST registration mandatory for freelancers?

Freelancers may need GST registration if turnover exceeds applicable limits or if specific GST provisions apply.

---

## Is GST registration mandatory for e-commerce sellers?

Many e-commerce sellers are required to register under GST irrespective of turnover limits.

---

## How long does GST registration take?

Typically, GST registration is processed within a few working days if all documents are correctly submitted.

---

# GST Rates FAQ

## What are the GST tax slabs in India?

Common GST slabs include:

* 0%
* 5%
* 12%
* 18%
* 28%

---

## Which GST rate is most common?

The 18% slab is one of the most commonly applied GST rates.

---

## How can I find the GST rate for a product?

GST rates are determined using HSN Codes for goods and SAC Codes for services.

---

# GST Invoice FAQ

## What is a GST invoice?

A GST invoice is a tax document issued by a registered supplier for the supply of goods or services.

---

## What details must be included in a GST invoice?

A GST invoice generally includes:

* Supplier details
* GSTIN
* Invoice number
* Invoice date
* Customer details
* Description of goods/services
* Taxable value
* GST rate
* Tax amount

---

## What is a Bill of Supply?

A Bill of Supply is issued when GST is not charged, such as under the Composition Scheme.

---

# Input Tax Credit (ITC) FAQ

## What is Input Tax Credit?

Input Tax Credit allows businesses to reduce their GST liability by claiming credit for GST paid on business purchases.

---

## Who can claim ITC?

Registered taxpayers satisfying GST conditions may claim eligible ITC.

---

## Can composition taxpayers claim ITC?

No. Businesses under the Composition Scheme cannot claim Input Tax Credit.

---

## What documents are required for ITC?

Generally:

* Tax invoice
* GST payment evidence
* Supplier compliance records

---

# GST Return Filing FAQ

## What is a GST return?

A GST return is a document that reports sales, purchases, tax liability, and tax payments.

---

## Why is GST return filing important?

Return filing ensures compliance and helps avoid penalties.

---

## What happens if returns are filed late?

Late filing may result in:

* Late fees
* Interest charges
* Compliance notices

---

## Can nil returns be filed?

Yes. Businesses with no transactions during a return period may file nil returns.

---

# GST Compliance FAQ

## What records must businesses maintain?

Businesses should maintain:

* Sales invoices
* Purchase invoices
* Tax payment records
* GST returns
* Accounting records

---

## How long should GST records be preserved?

Businesses should retain GST records for the period prescribed under GST regulations.

---

## What is GST reconciliation?

GST reconciliation involves comparing accounting records with GST returns to identify discrepancies.

---

# GST Refund FAQ

## What is a GST refund?

A GST refund is the repayment of excess GST paid or eligible GST credits.

---

## Who can claim GST refunds?

Eligible taxpayers including exporters and businesses with excess tax payments may apply for refunds.

---

## How long does a GST refund take?

Refund processing times vary depending on documentation and verification requirements.

---

# GST for Freelancers FAQ

## Do freelancers need GST?

Freelancers may require GST registration if turnover exceeds prescribed thresholds.

---

## Is GST applicable to foreign clients?

Exports of services may qualify as zero-rated supplies under GST.

---

## What is an LUT?

Letter of Undertaking (LUT) allows exporters to export services without paying GST.

---

# GST for Businesses FAQ

## What is the Composition Scheme?

A simplified GST scheme for eligible small businesses with lower compliance requirements.

---

## Can businesses switch from the Composition Scheme?

Yes, businesses can transition to the regular GST system subject to applicable conditions.

---

## Is GST beneficial for businesses?

GST improves tax transparency and enables eligible businesses to claim Input Tax Credit.

---

# GST Export & Import FAQ

## Is GST charged on exports?

Exports are generally treated as zero-rated supplies.

---

## Is GST applicable on imports?

Yes. Imports are generally subject to applicable GST and customs duties.

---

## Can exporters claim refunds?

Eligible exporters may claim refunds under GST provisions.

---

# GST Penalties FAQ

## What happens if GST is not paid?

Non-payment may result in:

* Interest
* Penalties
* Notices
* Recovery proceedings

---

## Can GST registration be cancelled?

Yes. GST registration may be cancelled under specific circumstances.

---

## How can penalties be avoided?

* File returns on time.
* Maintain proper records.
* Pay taxes accurately.
* Reconcile transactions regularly.

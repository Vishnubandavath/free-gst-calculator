# GST Guide for Businesses

Navigate the complexities of Indian taxation and turn GST compliance into a business advantage.

## Overview
Remaining compliant with GST is not just a legal requirement but a strategic asset. By understanding tax registration limits and input tax mechanics, small to medium enterprises can optimize cash flows and expand legally across state borders.

---

## Registration Thresholds

| Business Type | General States | Special Category States |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Suppliers of Goods** | ₹40 Lakhs | ₹20 Lakhs |
| **Service Providers** | ₹20 Lakhs | ₹10 Lakhs |

If your annual turnover crosses these limits, registering for a GSTIN is legally mandatory.

---

## The Composition Scheme

Small businesses with an annual turnover up to **₹1.5 crore** can opt for the **Composition Scheme**. 

*   **Fixed Low Rate**: Pay a flat tax rate (usually 1% to 5%) on your total turnover.
*   **Simple Filing**: File quarterly statements instead of monthly returns, reducing compliance overhead.
*   **Drawbacks**: You cannot claim Input Tax Credit (ITC), and you cannot issue tax invoices to charge GST to customers.
